My Bloody Valentine

My Bloody Valentine is a pioneering Irish-English band formed in 1983, known for their influential contributions to the shoegaze and alternative rock genres. Their landmark album 'Loveless,' released in 1991, is regarded as a seminal work in the shoegaze movement, pushing the boundaries of sonic experimentation. Despite a sporadic discography and prolonged hiatuses, My Bloody Valentine's impact on modern music remains profound, inspiring generations of musicians and earning them a devoted cult following worldwide.
